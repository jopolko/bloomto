How we picked these parcels

This list isn't a ranking. It's the parcels that pass every check we run for a 4-6 unit multiplex play under the post-Bill 185 rules. We start with all ~528,000 Toronto parcels and filter out anything a developer can't economically build on, then keep only the ones that meet a tight quality bar.

What makes the cut

A parcel has to check every one of these:

  • Walking distance to higher-order transit — within 500m of subway or streetcar. This is the Major Transit Station Area zone where the Bill 185 parking-minimum waiver applies and tenant demand is strongest.
  • Lot at least 350 m² — room for a 4-6 unit footprint without site-fitting compromise.
  • Zoning allows 4+ units as-of-right under the streamlined multiplex pathway (citywide 4-cap, or the Toronto–East York + Ward 23 sixplex carve-out from June 2025).
  • Clean teardown story — the existing building is detached, or the lot is vacant. Semis appear in the broader list; row-houses and unconfirmed structures are excluded everywhere.
  • Structure type confirmed by city data — a building-permit record, an OpenStreetMap building tag, or a Toronto Address Points cross-confirmation. Best-guess classifications don't make this list.

What you're seeing

~260 parcels citywide that pass every check. Not a ranking — a set of structurally clean teardown candidates within walking distance of higher-order transit, where the regulatory and economic pieces line up before you spend a minute underwriting.

What this isn't

It isn't financial advice. It isn't a guarantee that any specific parcel is for sale, or for sale at a price that pencils. And it isn't a complete pro forma — we surface the inputs that distinguish good parcels from bad, so you spend real time modelling the few worth modelling instead of all of them.

Worth knowing

  • The picks list refreshes weekly (Sundays). Daily signals in the Owner-Activity section — severance applications, demolition permits, property-standards orders — refresh every night from Toronto Open Data.
  • City building-geometry data has coverage gaps. We cross-check with Address Points and OpenStreetMap where possible, but verify on Google Street View before relying on any specific parcel's existing-structure claim.
  • Per-parcel construction-cost benchmarks in the detail panel come from declared values on building permits, which typically under-report actual hard costs by 20-40%. Treat them as a neighborhood-cost-tier benchmark, not a budget. Your cost consultant applies the multipliers.
